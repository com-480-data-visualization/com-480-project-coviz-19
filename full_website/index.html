Football Betting Data

The purpose of this website is showing different statistics from the major European fotball leagues with regards to betting. We discuss potential betting strategies and insights we found analyzing years worth of data

Team predictability

First we start of showing the most predictable teams in every country. The way we calculated this information, was by looking at the prediction the bookies had given to each match and comparing it with the final result. You can switch between country/year/betting provider.

Insights: You will notice that the most predictable teams are either the stronger teams or the weakest teams. The reason for this is that strong teams consitently win, and weak teams consinstently loose. So it is easier for bookies to predict the correct outcome in these situations

Map of countries with predictability

Similarly to the barchart above, we compute the percentage of times bookmakers predict the outcome of a match correctly, but this time we do it per country

Insights: As you will see inspecting the maps, on averege countries like England, Spain and especially Netherlands have the higher probability of being predicted correctly. There are however some exceptions, for example: In England in 2015 the percentage drops substantially due to the fact that Leicester City (a very averege team) managet to take home the championship

Accumulating chart with betting strategies

Now we will show some simulations of different betting strategions you could have followed through the years. The idea is the following: you decide that for an enire year, you will bet 10 CHF on each match of a footbal league, betting Win/Draw/Loose. You will have two strategies, either always bet on the least risky choice (which will probably help you win more often, but has a smaller coefficient of returns every time you win), or always go for the riskier choice (which will lead you to loose most of the time, but when you win, you win big).
Here you can try combinatins of different leagues/years.

Insights: Here is probably the most important point to take away from all the project. As you can see trying different combinations of the simulations, you will most of the time loose money. The bookies are experts at deriving perdictions and the coefficients thay set for every match are always carefully calculated that in the end, they get out as winners.
Even in the cases where you do win, you have to invest a really big amount of money for a fraction of the returns. The risk associated with this is really high.
In a nutshell: Betting is not good for you (or something like this, help please)
